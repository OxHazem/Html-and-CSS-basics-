Dr Ahmed Zewail

Nobel prize winner for chemistry in 1999

Early Life and Education

  • February 26, 1946 – Born in Damanhur, Egypt, and raised in Desouk, Egypt
  • 1967 – Earned a Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry from Alexandria University.
  • 1969 – Completed a Master’s degree in Chemistry from Alexandria University

Major Achievements and Awards:

  • 1999 – Won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for pioneering femtochemistry.
  • 2000 – Appointed as a US Science Envoy for the Middle East.
  • 2009 – Appointed to President Barack Obama’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST).
  • 2011 – Established the Zewail City of Science and Technology in Egypt.

Later Years and Death:

  • 2013 – Diagnosed with cancer but continued his work in science and education
  • August 2, 2016 – Passed away in the United States at the age of 70.
  • August 7, 2016 – His body was returned to Egypt and given a state funeral attended by President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi.
